Idea One

Gamification + Wearables + Smart Cities + Sports

Looking at a way to incentivize active living in communities

Idea:
Using wearable tech in social environments to gamify the game of basketball. With this product, a user can not only create pickup basketball games, but with the wearable tech in conjunction with smart city technology to track their stats in the game. These stats will be tied to a user’s account data, which will be displayed on leaderboards. Through incentivization by gamifying the sport, users will strive to improve their skills in the game, and in turn encourage them to be more and more active.

Project Analysis

If this was your topic, what would the goal of the project be?

The goal of this project is to create a social environment within community sports to help users join pickup basketball games. A common issue faced is not having someone or enough people to play with, and this project will be able to fix that. Another goal is to help users improve their skills by providing additional motivation through incentives via gamification, and creating a leaderboard where user’s progress will be displayed.

What do you like about this idea?

I like that this idea will not only encourage users to be more active, but it will help increase their social network. Meeting people to play sports with in an unorganized setting can be difficult, but with this product it can help ease people in.

What could potentially be your inspirations for this project?

The biggest inspiration I have for this project is from never having enough people to play pickup basketball games with. Often we would have an odd number of people and be forced to rotate with substitutions, leaving one person out. Another inspiration for this project is from the video game series NBA2k, a basketball simulation video game that incorporates game design into basketball skills, making users have to improve their abilities through an in game currency, providing “economic” incentives.

How do you imagine the general visual design of the project?

The general visual design for this project would follow a very information design based layout, focusing on delivering as much information as efficiently as possible to the user (where games are being played, average level of players, leaderboards, heat maps).

Existing related products:

HomeCourt
https://www.homecourt.ai/

“HomeCourt uses your iPhone or iPad to intelligently capture how you play—from your shots, to your dribbling, to your movements. By combining proven training concepts with real-time analysis, HomeCourt’s unique “train and play” model helps anyone get better at basketball. It’s the fastest way to develop basketball skills in a way that feels less like training and more like a video game.”

HomeCourt is an app developed to improve user’s basketball ability. HomeCourt provides the user with basic basketball drills and tracks the user’s ability to complete these drills. The app works by using the camera on the users phone to film the user doing premade basketball drills. Using AI technology, the camera tracks the user's movements as well as location on the court to determine their ability on the court. If they shoot 4 three-point shots but only make one, then the app will record the stats, and their three-point percentage for the workout will be 25%. The app saves the heatmap of the user’s shooting ability on the court and then provides analytics based on their workout, as well as methods to improve their ability. The only technology this app uses is the camera on the user’s phone, no external device is used to track data. The app also features “Challenges”, usually with cash prizes, creating an economic incentive for users.

ShotTracker
https://shottracker.com/

“ShotTracker is a revolutionary sensor-based system that delivers statistics and analytics to teams, fans and broadcast networks instantly, enhancing the experience of the game — both on and off the court.”

ShotTracker is a digital product that specializes in track a user’s ability on the basketball court. Despite the name, it tracks more than just shots; the app tracks all stats on the court, such as points, rebounds, assists, steals, blocks, and turnovers. It works by tracking the user with sensors around the court as well as one on the user playing. The goal of this app is to help the user improve their skills in the game of basketball, as well as for coaches to understand their strengths and areas that need improvement on their team.

HoopMaps
https://apps.apple.com/us/app/hoop-maps-find-pickup-basketball-games/id1087164059

“Don't waste your time playing on an empty court. See when pickup games are happening and join games to meet more people.”

HoopMaps is an app that allows users to organize pickup basketball games, indoors and outdoors. By creating its own social space as well as being connected with social media, users can create and share pickup basketball games, invite friends, and message friends through the platform about games you want to play in.

Idea Two


Smart Cities + Navigation

How to get people to leave you alone

Idea:
My second idea is creating an anti-social walking wayfinding app. With this product, users can input their location, input their destination, and based on location data crowd-sourced by smart city sensors, the app will determine the path with the least amount of people.

Project Analysis

If this was your topic, what would the goal of the project be?

The goal of this project is to help streamline foot traffic through densely populated areas. Navigating through crowds can be difficult, especially when you are already walking with a group. If time is not an issue, finding the path of least resistance is beneficial to people who dislike crowds.

What do you like about this idea?

I like that this idea will help streamline foot traffic in major cities like Toronto. When population outgrows the existing infrastructure, solutions need to be put in place to reduce overcrowding.

What could potentially be your inspirations for this project?

My inspiration for this idea comes from my own dislike of walking through crowds. I often take the long way to get to places if it means dealing with less people.

How do you imagine the general visual design of the project?

TThe general visual design for this project would follow a map based visual style akin to apps like Uber, Google Maps, Lyft, Pokemon Go, etc.

Existing related products:

Waze
https://www.waze.com/

Waze is a wayfinding app that uses real-time data from drivers to determine the best possible path for their trip. It notifies the user if there are traffic delays, police in the area, and other roadblocks.
